Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to buy or bring anything?

Yes — you will need to bring a National Mah Jongg League 2026 Rules and Hands card. Cards are $14 on the official NMJL website: nationalmahjonggleague.org/store.aspx.

We provide everything else you need to play. Just bring yourself and your card, and we'll take care of the rest!

How many people can you teach at a private lesson?

Private lessons can accommodate groups of varying sizes. Contact us to discuss your group and we'll put together the perfect experience for you.

If I already have a mahjong set, can we use it during our lesson?

Absolutely! We love when students bring their own sets — it's a great way to get comfortable with the tiles you'll be playing with at home. Just let us know in advance.

Can beginners attend open play events?

Yes! You do not need much experience to attend open play, but please make sure you've had at least one beginner class and have a basic understanding of the game. We place beginners together and there is always a coach to help when needed. It's a wonderful way to learn in a relaxed setting.

Do you offer Corporate and/or Fundraising events?

Yes! Corporate mahjong events make for a fantastic and memorable team-building activity. Fundraising events are a great way to raise funds for our community, and we would love to help you do that. Reach out to discuss packages, group size, and availability.

I just booked my lesson. What's next?

You'll receive a confirmation email with all the details. Just show up at the scheduled time — we'll handle everything else. We can't wait to play with you!

I need to cancel or reschedule. What is your policy?

Please contact us at least 24 hours before your scheduled session for a reschedule or refund. Late cancellations may be subject to a fee or loss of open play payment. If you can find someone to take your place, we can transfer your spot to them — always reach out and we'll do our best to work with you.
