## Design Context

### Users
Adults 70+ in Norman, OK discovering a local mahjong studio. They browse on desktop or tablet, likely in the daytime. Their job: find out if this is a welcoming place, understand what classes are offered, and book a session. Emotions to evoke: warmth, ease, quiet delight — like stepping into a well-appointed social club for the first time.

### Brand Personality
Welcoming, Approachable, Social. A country club feel without the velvet rope — refined enough to feel special, warm enough that anyone feels they belong. The approved brand guide is the source of truth: forest green, ivory, gold, blush, Cinzel display, Playfair Display italic body, and the sun-tile motif.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light theme (warm ivory dominant). Rich forest green for contrast sections. Gold used sparingly as an accent — its rarity is what makes it feel luxurious. The graphic artist's brand guide (client-approved) establishes: layered type hierarchy (Cinzel all-caps + Playfair italic script), the sun/flower tile as a visual signature, and business-card-quality polish. Nothing trendy. Nothing that would look dated in 3 years.

**Anti-references**: No tech-startup feel. No youthful, loud, or energetic. Not a restaurant menu site. Not anything that makes it feel like a product page — this is a community, not a service.

**Client constraints**: Color palette and font choices are locked (client-approved). Overall layout and page flow are approved. Changes should refine quality, not alter direction.

### Design Principles
1. **Warmth over sharpness** — every detail should feel like hand-lettered stationery, not a grid system. Generous spacing, soft borders, gentle transitions.
2. **Gold is precious** — use it only for accents, labels, and single emphasis points. Overuse kills it.
3. **The tile is the brand** — the sun-flower tile motif from the brand guide is the visual signature. Use it where icons are needed instead of emoji or generic shapes.
4. **Large and legible** — 70+ audience. Default to larger than you think. Never sacrifice readability for aesthetics.
5. **Quiet luxury** — nothing flashy, nothing discordant. Refinement comes from restraint: consistent spacing, precise typography, smooth transitions.
